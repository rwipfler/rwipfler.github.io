Creative & Community

artists · field artifacts · deep-sea visual culture

This page collects both the artists I keep returning to and some of the field-adjacent things I have made, worn, printed, or photographed. It is not meant to be exhaustive. It is a curated record of the visual culture that shapes how I think about the deep ocean and how I share it with other people.

The format here is deliberate: each artist entry includes the creator, what they make, and why I keep it around. Below that, the project section works more like a field-artifact shelf, with room for the weirder and more personal things that still belong to the same deep-sea world.

Creator work
In my office
Biodiversity illustration

Zoe Keller

Maine-based nature artist working in detailed pencil and digital drawings that explore biodiversity and the interconnectedness of fragile ecosystems. Her ocean biodiversity work includes deep-sea species such as barreleyes, vampire squid, siphonophores, and atolla jellies.

Why I keep coming back: the compositions feel like systems thinking in image form, which fits the way I think about the ocean.

Best card use: one full biodiversity composition plus one photo of a print or book page in your office.

Marine wildlife art

NocturnalSea

A traditional marine and wildlife artist based primarily out of Connecticut, with work centered on ocean life and natural-history-style species art. This one pushes more toward classic wildlife illustration than merch-first branding.

Why I keep coming back: the work has a clean marine-life focus and gives the page another mode besides shirts and papercraft.

Papercraft

Sneepsnorp

Large-format deep-sea papercraft builds, including bigfin squid and giant isopod designs, with a strong sense of structure and creature personality.

Why I keep coming back: the work makes odd deep-sea animals feel tangible and engineered, not just illustrated.
