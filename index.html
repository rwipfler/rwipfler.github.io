I'm a PhD candidate in Geological & Planetary Sciences at Caltech, working in the Orphan Lab under Dr. Victoria Orphan. My research focuses on in-situ microbial activity in the deep ocean — the invisible ecosystems sustained by chemistry rather than sunlight.

Before the deep sea pulled me in, I was hiking into Yellowstone's backcountry to collect thermophilic microbes from boiling springs — a different kind of extreme environment, and where the obsession with chemosynthesis began. I'm also an Ocean Exploration Trust alumni, having joined E/V Nautilus first as an intern and later as Science Manager in Training.
